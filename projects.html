Firmware & Tooling

BLT latency measurement platform

Better Latency Tester (BLT) is a low-latency measurement rig that captures click-to-photon timings for PC games. Built around an STM32 Nucleo board, it blends USB HID emulation, DMA-driven ADC sampling, and microsecond timing to produce reproducible latency data.

  • Automatic or manual light-threshold calibration with photodiode and mic trigger modes.
  • Batch USB-triggered runs with onboard logging, Python GUI, and CSV exports.
  • Sample CS2 data shows Reflex shaving ~3 ms at 240 Hz; ideal for comparative testing.

Architecture

ARMv8 microarchitecture study

Implemented an ARM-style RISC CPU in SystemVerilog, moving from single-cycle core to 5-stage pipeline with hazard detection and forwarding. Integrated a direct-mapped cache and used ModelSim benches to measure CPI impacts from branch penalties and cache misses.

Validation

Air quality monitor

Developed a CO₂/temperature/humidity monitor around the SCD41 sensor with SD card logging via FatFs. Designed the SPI storage pipeline, handled calibration, and built a minimal UI for quick analytics.

Systems

Bare-metal Cortex-M0+

Crafted bare-metal firmware for the STM32G031: custom linker scripts, startup files, and peripheral configuration without vendor HALs. Flashed/debugged with OpenOCD while characterising timer, GPIO, and clock behaviour.

Performance

PC extreme overclocking

Reprogrammed PWM controllers over I²C to overvolt GPU core/memory rails, measured stability with scopes, and tuned decoupling capacitance. Documented findings to inform firmware-level power management experiments.
