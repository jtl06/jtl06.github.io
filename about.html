About me

I’m an Electrical and Computer Engineering student at the University of Washington, focused on low-level systems that bridge silicon and software. My time spans sensor science at NIST and building custom keyboards through Aeus, the boutique company I founded to explore firmware, hardware, and manufacturing partnerships.

What I do

I design firmware, embedded tooling, and measurement platforms. Recent highlights include a high-density temperature monitoring system for NIST’s tape tunnel chamber and the Better Latency Tester (BLT) — a click-to-photon benchmarking rig built on STM32. I also run Aeus Keyboards, delivering bespoke builds and firmware in C for ATMega32U4-based controllers.

How I work

I’m pragmatic but detail-oriented: start with measured requirements, prototype quickly, instrument everything, and write lean documentation. Whether I’m coordinating cross-division reviews at NIST or negotiating overseas production for Aeus, I keep feedback loops short and transparency high.

Experience

  • NIST Sensor Science Division · 2023 — Built a Raspberry Pi data acquisition platform with 61 I²C sensors, uncovering five hotspot regions and cutting chamber temperature uncertainty by 38%.
  • Aeus Keyboards · 2020 – Present — Founded a custom keyboard studio (> $20K revenue), designing ATMega32U4 PCBs, writing C firmware, managing overseas manufacturing, and hand-assembling SMD prototypes.

Skills snapshot

C/C++, ARM & RISC-V assembly, Python, SystemVerilog, MATLAB; Git/GitHub, GCC, GDB, Quartus, Vivado, ModelSim; USB FS, DMA-driven ADC, UART/I²C/SPI/GPIO, timers/ISRs, JTAG, CRC, Zephyr RTOS, Linux/WSL.

Beyond the desk

Outside of labs and firmware repos, I compete in keyboard design contests, tinker with audio, and mentor peers getting started with embedded development.
