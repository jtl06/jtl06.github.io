About me

I’m a college student focused on firmware and computer architecture engineering. I’m happiest reverse engineering protocols, tuning toolchains, and eliminating the unknowns between silicon and software. When the lab lights are off, you’ll probably find me hand-wiring a new keyboard layout.

What I do

My work spans firmware bring-up, microcontroller peripherals, memory hierarchies, and performance profiling. I thrive in spaces where I can validate assumptions with instrumentation and iterate quickly using tight feedback loops.

How I work

I’m pragmatic but detail-oriented: start with clear requirements, build observable systems, write lean docs, and automate the boring stuff. That mix keeps projects approachable for collaborators and future me alike.

Beyond the desk

Outside of labs and firmware repos, I compete in keyboard design contests, tinker with audio, and mentor peers getting started with embedded development.
