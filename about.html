Arm — Incoming Software Intern, 2026

The role centers on Linux-based profiling, benchmarking, and PMU/perf-style measurement workflows — the kind of low-level performance work I’ve been chasing since I first started timing code paths on STM32.

NIST Sensor Science Division — Intern, 2023

At NIST I built a 61-node I²C temperature sensor network on Raspberry Pi to profile thermal gradients inside the Tape Tunnel test chamber. The system replaced manual spot-checks with continuous automated monitoring, which uncovered five previously unknown hotspot regions and let the team reduce temperature-induced measurement uncertainty by 38% through targeted procedure updates. I also wrote the calibration routines for the division’s legacy sensor infrastructure.

Aeus Keyboards — Founder, 2020–2025

I started Aeus Keyboards in high school and grew it to over K in revenue across 40+ custom builds for clients worldwide. I handled everything end-to-end: designing cases in CAD, laying out ATmega32U4 PCBs in KiCad, writing QMK firmware (matrix scanning, debouncing, USB HID), coordinating CNC machining with overseas manufacturers, and hand-soldering SMD prototypes. Running a small hardware business taught me how to manage timelines, communicate with clients, and ship physical products that have to be right the first time.
